
# 11 - Manager Mindset

> Version: 1.3

# Purpose

The CISSP exam does not simply test technical knowledge.
It tests whether you can make sound security decisions as a security leader.

---

# Principle 1 - Think Business First

Wrong question:

> How do I fix this vulnerability?

Correct question:

> Which action best reduces business risk?

---

# Principle 2 - Follow the Lifecycle

When multiple answers are technically correct, prefer the one that follows the proper process.

```text
Identify
   │
Assess
   │
Analyze
   │
Decide
   │
Implement
   │
Verify
```

Do not jump directly to implementation.

---

# Principle 3 - Prefer Prevention

Priority order:

1. Prevent
2. Detect
3. Respond
4. Recover

If prevention is a realistic option, it is often the strongest CISSP answer.

---

# Principle 4 - Respect Governance

Always consider:

- Policies
- Standards
- Procedures
- Legal requirements
- Authorization

Example:

Never begin a penetration test without authorization and Rules of Engagement.

---

# Principle 5 - Evidence Before Opinion

Managers make decisions using evidence.

Prefer answers that:

- Collect data
- Measure
- Validate
- Document

before changing systems.

---

# Principle 6 - Risk-Based Decisions

Not every vulnerability deserves the same response.

Consider:

- Business impact
- Likelihood
- Criticality
- Cost
- Residual risk

---

# Principle 7 - Least Disruptive Effective Action

Avoid unnecessary operational impact.

Choose solutions that achieve the objective while minimizing business disruption.

---

# Common CISSP Decision Tree

```text
Is there authorization?
        │
        ▼
Understand the risk?
        │
        ▼
Collect evidence?
        │
        ▼
Choose the control?
        │
        ▼
Implement?
        │
        ▼
Verify effectiveness?
```

---

# Frequent Exam Patterns

| Scenario | Usually Best Thinking |
|----------|------------------------|
| Multiple technical fixes | Choose the one that best manages risk |
| Immediate exploit | Contain first, then investigate |
| Missing information | Gather evidence before acting |
| Compliance question | Think audit and governance |
| Executive audience | Communicate business impact |

---

# Final Checklist

Before selecting an answer ask:

- Is it authorized?
- Does it reduce risk?
- Does it follow process?
- Is evidence available?
- Does it align with business objectives?

If two answers seem correct, the one that better satisfies these questions is usually the CISSP answer.

---

# 30-Second Revision

- Think like a security leader.
- Business before technology.
- Risk before tools.
- Process before action.
- Evidence before assumptions.
- Governance before implementation.
