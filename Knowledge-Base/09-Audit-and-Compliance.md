---

# Why Perform an Audit?

An audit answers:

> **"Are we complying with defined requirements?"**

Requirements may come from:

- Laws
- Regulations
- Contracts
- Internal policies
- Security standards

Unlike a penetration test, an audit is **not** trying to exploit systems.

# Internal vs External Audit

| Internal Audit | External Audit |
|---------------|----------------|
| Performed by the organization or its internal audit function | Performed by an independent third party |
| Improves internal controls | Provides independent assurance |
| Continuous improvement focus | Stakeholder confidence and compliance |

---

# Evidence

Good audit evidence should be:

- Objective
- Reliable
- Relevant
- Sufficient
- Repeatable

Examples:

- Configuration files
- Change records
- Access logs
- Policies
- Interview records

---

# Sampling

Auditors rarely examine every record.

Sampling allows conclusions to be drawn efficiently while balancing cost and confidence.

---

# Findings

A finding should include:

- Observation
- Evidence
- Risk
- Recommendation

Avoid reporting problems without actionable recommendations.

---

# SOC Reports (Service Organization Control)

At the CISSP level:

| Report | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| SOC 1 | Controls affecting financial reporting |
| SOC 2 | Security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, privacy |
| SOC 3 | Public summary of SOC 2 results |

Know **when** each report is appropriate rather than memorizing every detail.

---

# CISSP Exam Traps

| If the question says... | Think... |
|-------------------------|----------|
| Verify compliance | Audit |
| Independent assurance | External audit |
| Objective proof | Evidence |
| Review a subset | Sampling |
| Cloud provider assurance | SOC report |
